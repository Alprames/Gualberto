Gualberto Ibarreto

Also known as 'El Pichón' and 'El Cantor del Pueblo'

Over the years, Gualberto...

  • 1947— Was born in El Pilar, Sucre, Venezuela on the 12th of July
  • 1972— Joined the choir project of Escuela Briceño Méndez and also started playing the mandolin in the band from that same school
  • 1973— In June, he performed two consecutive nights in the concert hall of the Central University of Venezuela and, that same month, he was invited to the House of Culture of El Tigre, where he performed for the first time as a folk music singer.
  • 1974— Won the first place in the 1st Universitary Festival from the ULA after performing the song Cerecita composed by Luis Mariano Rivera
  • 1975— Released his first solo LP, called Gualberto Ibarreto which included the hits María Antonia and Ah cuerpo cobarde with which he won the award El Mara de Oro.
  • 1984— Collaborates with the folkloric band Serenata Guayanesa in the recording of Recuerda Morena, song included in the LP Viene la parranda
  • 1993— He struggled in the 1990s to overcome his substance abuse problem, and, successfully copes with his alcoholism, making a comeback. His comeback album: Hoy, he vuelto a ser became an instant hit in the Venezuelan market.
  • 2011— In November 2011, the National Assembly gave him the title of living cultural heritage of Venezuela, for being a popular cultist.
  • 2013— In more than forty years of musical career, Gualberto José Ibarreto Barrios has toured all the stages of the country. His musical training began in a self-taught way with the influence of popular musicians from eastern Venezuela.
  • 2020— The 'Gualberto Ibarreto' boulevard located in the town of El Peñón within the city of Cumaná, capital of the state where he is from, is inaugurated in homage to his cultural legacy.
When you sing alone, you sing differently, you sing for yourself. Whatever song it is, because in the end the songs belong to no one, they only belong to whoever is singing them.

— Gualberto Ibarreto (2020)
